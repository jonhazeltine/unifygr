Update on the Land

Where things stand with the possible sale of a portion of our property to the Leo Brown Group — and the decisions still ahead of us.

Nothing is final. We have only signed a letter of intent — an agreement to explore in good faith. New Life decides at each step below, and we are not committed to sell unless a full agreement protects our facility's room to grow.
A formal step or decision A money moment A protection for our community
SIGNED JULY 1, 2026 · WHERE WE ARE TODAY Confirmed

A signed letter of intent

On July 1, 2026, our board voted unanimously to sign a letter of intent with the Leo Brown Group — an agreement, in good faith, to explore a full purchase agreement together. This is a firm first step, but a starting point, not a sale. The Leo Brown Group hopes to build a senior-living community on the land; New Life would keep the parcel with our facility and room to expand. Right now New Life is beginning to engage the professionals we need — including our attorney — to confirm our future plans fit on the land we would keep, while their team drafts a proposed agreement for us to consider.

Agreement to explore, in good faith $5,000 good-faith deposit — refundable for now
THE NEXT DECISION · AUGUST 2026 AT THE LATEST Approximate

Whether to sign a full purchase agreement

This is a choice, not a foregone conclusion. If — and only if — the terms protect our community, New Life would agree to turn the letter into a full, detailed contract. That agreement is where the protections for our retained land are written in: where we can expand, shared driveways and parking, and how the land is divided. If those protections aren't there, we don't sign.

A decision point for New Life A $50,000 earnest-money deposit would follow — refundable through the study period
IF WE SIGN · ABOUT AUGUST 2026 – EARLY 2027 Approximate

A study & City approval period would begin

The Leo Brown Group would study the land — title, survey, environmental, utilities — and apply to Grand Rapids Township to approve their plan. As part of that same application, and at their cost, they would include our facility's future expansion. Meanwhile the land would be formally surveyed and divided so our portion becomes its own parcel. During this period the first portions of their deposits also become committed — non-refundable to them, a sign of their seriousness — though every committed dollar still counts toward the purchase price if a sale closes.

Survey, division & Township filing $5,000 commits once the land is confirmed developable $25,000 of the earnest money commits at Township filing
OUR KEY SAFEGUARD

Our expansion must be approved before any sale can close

Grand Rapids Township would have to give conceptual approval to our facility's future growth — sanctuary, daycare, and parking — before any sale is allowed to close. If our plans can't be protected, New Life is not obligated to go through with it. This is the promise that keeps a deal from ever boxing in our ministry.

Condition to closing
THE GO / NO-GO DECISION · AROUND FEBRUARY 2027 Approximate

Both sides decide whether to proceed

After the study period, the Leo Brown Group decides whether to move forward or step away — and New Life continues to hold its protections. If they continue, the rest of their earnest money becomes non-refundable (still credited toward the purchase price). Up to this point, if the land doesn't work, either side can walk away and our community is no worse off.

Remaining earnest money commits — credited toward purchase Any extension: $35,000 each, also credited
ONLY IF EVERY CONDITION IS MET · MID-2027 AT THE EARLIEST Approximate

A closing would complete the sale

A sale closes only if every condition — including protecting our expansion — has been met. If it does, ownership of the sold portion transfers and New Life receives the agreed purchase price, approximately $2.5 million. Our retained parcel and its protections (room to expand, shared access, and parking) are permanently recorded so they carry forward for good.

Ownership transfers, rights recorded Purchase price received (~$2.5M)
AFTER A CLOSING

Transition & good-neighbor arrangements

If a sale is completed, site cleanup would be handled by the Leo Brown Group at their own cost, and shared access and parking arrangements would take effect for both our facility and the new senior-living community next door — Sunday mornings and church events paired with their quieter weekday hours.

Shared arrangements begin
IF BUILT · LATE 2027 – EARLY 2028 Estimate

Estimated start of construction

These next two dates are estimates only, based on typical timelines for a project like this — not commitments. If a sale closes, the Leo Brown Group would finish final engineering and pull Township building permits, then break ground on site work and building. For a community of this size, that would likely begin several months after a closing — roughly late 2027 to early 2028.

Estimate · based on market norms
IF BUILT · 2029 – 2030 Estimate

Estimated completion & opening

A senior-living community of roughly 160 units — including memory care — built on undeveloped land typically takes about 18 to 24 months to construct. That points to an estimated opening somewhere around 2029 to 2030. Like the date above, this is illustrative and would shift with weather, permitting, financing, and final design.
